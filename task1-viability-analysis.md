# Task 1: Campaign Viability Analysis — GlowNest

## Brand Overview

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Product | Premium Face Serum |
| Selling Price | ₹800 |
| COGS | ₹280 |
| Amazon Referral Fee | 12% |
| Monthly Ad Spend | ₹3,00,000 |
| Units via Ads | 1,500/month |
| Organic Units | 2,000/month |
| Fixed Costs | ₹1,50,000/month |

---

## Q1: Unit Economics (Ad-Attributed Sales)

| Metric | Calculation | Value |
|---|---|---|
| Gross Margin | ₹800 − ₹280 | ₹520/unit |
| Amazon Referral Fee | ₹800 × 12% | ₹96/unit |
| Ad Cost per Unit | ₹3,00,000 ÷ 1,500 | ₹200/unit |
| Net Margin (Ad Units) | ₹520 − ₹96 − ₹200 | **₹224/unit** |

**Interpretation:** Even after ad costs, each ad-driven sale yields
₹224 profit. Ads are not eating into profitability — this is a
healthy unit economics profile for premium skincare.

---

## Q2: Monthly Profitability

| Stream | Calculation | Value |
|---|---|---|
| Net Margin — Organic | ₹520 − ₹96 | ₹424/unit |
| Profit from Ad Units | ₹224 × 1,500 | ₹3,36,000 |
| Profit from Organic Units | ₹424 × 2,000 | ₹8,48,000 |
| Gross Profit | ₹3,36,000 + ₹8,48,000 | ₹11,84,000 |
| Less: Fixed Costs | | −₹1,50,000 |
| **Monthly Net Profit** | | **₹10,34,000** |

**Interpretation:** GlowNest is comfortably profitable. Organic sales
contribute more than double the profit of ad-driven sales — a strong
sign of brand health. The business is not dependent on ads to survive.

---

## Q3: ACOS and TACOS

| Metric | Formula | Value |
|---|---|---|
| Ad Revenue | ₹800 × 1,500 | ₹12,00,000 |
| Total Revenue | ₹800 × 3,500 | ₹28,00,000 |
| ACOS | ₹3,00,000 ÷ ₹12,00,000 × 100 | **25%** |
| TACOS | ₹3,00,000 ÷ ₹28,00,000 × 100 | **10.7%** |

**ACOS 25% — Healthy.**
Net margin per ad unit is ₹224 (positive). Break-even ACOS on ad
units alone is 53%, so there is significant headroom before ad
spend becomes a problem.

**TACOS 10.7% — Excellent.**
Industry benchmark for healthy brands is below 15%. At 10.7%,
organic sales are carrying significant weight. GlowNest is building
real search equity, not just renting visibility through paid ads.

---

## Q4: Break-Even Ad Spend

At break-even, Total Monthly Profit = 0.

**Working:**
- Organic profit (fixed): ₹424 × 2,000 = ₹8,48,000
- Ad unit profit: (₹424 × 1,500) − Ad Spend = ₹6,36,000 − X
- Fixed costs: ₹1,50,000
- Equation: ₹8,48,000 + ₹6,36,000 − X − ₹1,50,000 = 0
- **X = ₹13,34,000**

**Maximum ad spend = ₹13,34,000/month**
Corresponding ACOS = ₹13,34,000 ÷ ₹12,00,000 = **111%**

*Note: This is high because organic profits heavily subsidize losses.
On a standalone ad-unit basis, break-even ACOS = ₹424 ÷ ₹800 = 53%.*

## Q5: Scenario — ACOS Worsens to 40%, Units Drop to 1,200

| Metric | Calculation | Value |
|---|---|---|
| Ad Spend at 40% ACOS | 40% × (₹800 × 1,200) | ₹3,84,000 |
| Ad Cost per Unit | ₹3,84,000 ÷ 1,200 | ₹320/unit |
| Net Margin per Ad Unit | ₹520 − ₹96 − ₹320 | ₹104/unit |
| Profit from Ad Units | ₹104 × 1,200 | ₹1,24,800 |
| Profit from Organic | ₹424 × 2,000 | ₹8,48,000 |
| Total − Fixed Costs | ₹9,72,800 − ₹1,50,000 | **₹8,22,800** |

**Impact:** Profit drops ₹10,34,000 → ₹8,22,800 (−₹2.11L). Still
profitable but deteriorating fast.

**Recommendations:**
1. Pause all broad-match campaigns — primary cause of ACOS spikes
2. Run a full negative keyword audit immediately
3. Shift budget to exact-match on top 10-15 converting keywords
4. Do NOT cut total spend aggressively — organic ranking will drop
5. Review product listing images and copy — poor creative hurts
   conversion rate and mechanically worsens ACOS

---

## Q6: Scenario — Double Ad Spend to ₹6,00,000, 2,500 Units

| Metric | Calculation | Value |
|---|---|---|
| Ad Cost per Unit | ₹6,00,000 ÷ 2,500 | ₹240/unit |
| Net Margin per Ad Unit | ₹520 − ₹96 − ₹240 | ₹184/unit |
| Profit from Ad Units | ₹184 × 2,500 | ₹4,60,000 |
| Profit from Organic | ₹424 × 2,000 | ₹8,48,000 |
| Total − Fixed Costs | ₹13,08,000 − ₹1,50,000 | **₹11,58,000** |
| TACOS | ₹6,00,000 ÷ ₹40,00,000 | **15%** |

**vs Current:** Profit increases ₹10,34,000 → ₹11,58,000 (+₹1,24,000)

**Decision: Conditionally Yes.**

The math supports scaling — net profit rises by ₹1.24L/month.
However:
- Per-unit efficiency drops: ₹224 → ₹184 net margin (diminishing returns)
- TACOS hits exactly 15% — the upper boundary of healthy
- Any organic softness or ACOS increase will push TACOS into danger

**Recommendation:** Run the scale-up for 4-6 weeks. Monitor whether
additional ad spend improves organic keyword rankings (which brings
TACOS back down). Set a hard stop rule: if TACOS exceeds 17%,
roll back spend to ₹4,50,000.
