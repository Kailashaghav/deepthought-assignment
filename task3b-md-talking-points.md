# Task 3B: MD Talking Points — UrbanPulse (Men's Grooming)

**Context:** 15-minute call with the MD to discuss Amazon growth
plans for next quarter.

**Why UrbanPulse:** Most strategically interesting account.
Underperforming on paper (ROAS 2.1) but with clear, fixable levers
that MD-level buy-in could unlock — listing investment, channel
patience, and an untested Flipkart opportunity.

---

## Opening (First 2 minutes)

"I wanted to give you a picture of where the Amazon channel stands
and where I see the growth coming from next quarter — not campaign
metrics, but the actual business opportunity."

Current state: Month 2 on Amazon advertising. Revenue is building.
The algorithm is in its learning phase — this is normal and expected
for the first 90 days. The foundation being built now (keyword
structure, campaign architecture) is what organic ranking is built
on 6 months from now.

---

## What the Data Is Telling Us

**1. The category is growing.**
Men's grooming on Amazon India is one of the faster-growing
categories. Being active now — even during the learning phase —
is building search equity. The question is who builds it faster,
you or a competitor.

**2. Listing quality is the bottleneck, not ad spend.**
Top 3 competitors have stronger product images and A+ content.
This suppresses conversion rate — even when your ad wins the
placement, fewer customers click through or buy. A listing upgrade
on your top 2 SKUs could improve results without any increase
in ad spend.

**3. ROAS will recover by Month 3-4 if we stay the course.**
Month 1 ROAS comparisons with previous agencies are misleading.
Many inflate Month 1 with aggressive broad-match spend that burns
budget on irrelevant searches. What we're building is slower but
produces sustainable, compounding returns.

---

## Growth Opportunities for Next Quarter

**Move 1: Listing Upgrade (Highest ROI, Lowest Cost)**
Updated lifestyle photography and A+ content on top 2 SKUs.
Expected impact: 10-20% improvement in conversion rate,
which improves ROAS without any additional ad spend.

**Move 2: Flipkart as a Second Channel**
Your Flipkart store is live but completely unadvertised.
Competitors are already there. Flipkart's ad platform is less
mature — lower cost-per-click, less keyword competition.
A ₹1L/month test budget could validate ₹3-5L/month in
incremental revenue.

**Move 3: Sponsored Brand Video (Once ROAS Stabilises)**
Most effective format for capturing new-to-brand customers
in men's grooming. This is how brands move from #4 to #2
in subcategory rankings.

---

## Questions for the MD

1. What is the topline target for Amazon over the next 6 months —
   is this a primary growth channel or complementary to retail/D2C?

2. Is there budget and bandwidth to refresh product photography
   this quarter? This may be the single highest-leverage move
   available right now.

3. Is there appetite to run a Flipkart advertising pilot —
   even ₹1L/month to test the channel?

4. How does your team measure Amazon success — ROAS, topline
   revenue, or subcategory market share?

---
